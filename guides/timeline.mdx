The timeline is located above the graph. It lets you explore how the campaign unfolded over time by filtering the graph to a specific time range or replaying events in sequence.

## What you can do with the timeline

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Filter by time range" icon="calendar-range">
    Narrow the graph display to show only activity within a selected time window.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Replay events" icon="play">
    Display the graph as events occurred chronologically, stepping through the campaign from start to finish.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fast forward / reverse" icon="forward">
    Accelerate or reverse playback to move quickly through longer campaigns.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Jump to a time" icon="clock">
    Drag the scrubber to jump directly to a specific point in the campaign timeline.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Timezone settings

By default, the timeline displays times in your current local timezone. You can change this in the **Settings Modal**:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Settings Modal">
    Click the settings icon in the navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Timezone section">
    Locate the **Timezone** setting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable AutoSelect">
    Uncheck the **AutoSelect** option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a timezone">
    Use the dropdown to choose your desired timezone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Changing the timezone only affects how timestamps are displayed. The underlying campaign data is not modified.
</Info>

## How the timeline affects the graph

As you move the timeline scrubber, the graph updates to reflect only the hosts and beacons that were active during the selected time window. Beacons are categorized per time bucket as:

- **Created** — Beacons that came online during the interval
- **Active** — Beacons active throughout the entire interval
- **Dying** — Beacons that went offline during the interval
- **Dead** — Beacons that no longer exist by this point in time

This makes it straightforward to reconstruct the sequence of events during a campaign and identify when specific hosts and beacons were involved.
